Heat water to 96 C. Use filtered water. If available, dissolve one Third Wave Water (TM) packet into a gallon of distilled water.

Grind setting numbers are for a Baratza Encore.

...

I usually use the "inverted method"

Setup:

  • Plunger on bottom, reservoir on top
  • Circular filter in screen, wetted
  1. Grind:
    1. # 15, 20g. Tare scale.
  2. Water:
    1. 50g bloom, stir.
    2. Wait till 0:30
    3. Fill to 150g total
    4. Wait till 1:30
  3. Pour:
    1. Attach the black screen + filter to the brewer. Turn + lock it in.
    2. Invert quickly and press into cup
      1. Don't stress, the filter will hold the water in.
      2. Hold cup with free hand to make sure you don't push it off the table
    3. Add hot water or ice until dilute enough

Cleanup:

  • Plunge puck of grounds + filter into the trash
  • Rinse brewer + filter screen

Chemex
...

Setup:

  • Largest white paper filter
    • These are just big pieces of filter paper folded into quarters
    • We partially unfold the layers to make a cone: 3 layers on one side, 1 layer on the other
  • Filter goes in chemex, 3-layer side toward the spout
  • Wet the filter a bit to get it to stay down. If you use hot water, it also preheats the carafe.
  • Pour out this water.
  1. Grind (double the mass for two people):
    1. # 14, 18g. Tare scale.
  2. Water (double the mass for two people):
    1. 50g bloom, stir. This is important for Chemex.
    2. Wait till 0:30
    3. Fill to 150g total
    4. Wait till 1:30
    5. Fill to 250g total
    6. Wait till 2:30
    7. Fill to 300g total
  3. Pour:
    1. Wait for Chemex to drain.
      1. Could take a while. If it seems really slow, you can lift one edge of the filter a bit to help it drain. Bad design.
    2. Dump the filter, or hold the grinds back with your hand when pouring
    3. Pour out the spout

Cleanup:

  • Grounds + filter into the trash
  • Rinse brewer
    • Spin the brewer + quickly invert over the sink to rinse the edges of the cone
  • Usually also needs a wash + rinse with soap, especially if left with coffee inside

Espresso
...

Setup:

  • ~5 min before using, turn the machine on (leftmost button)
    • This preheats the boiler and group head
  • Check the water reservoir level
    • Use ONLY filtered water, ideally only Third Wave Water mix + distilled water
    • Running the machine dry is not good for it
  • Check the drip tray level - a yellow flag will pop up when full
    • Pull it out slowly and dump it in the sink
    • Rinse it out with water
  1. Grind:
    1. # 0, 14-15g
      1. If drip coffee was ground beforehand, some coarser grinds remain in the grinder. Toss the first ~5g of these.
      2. Take the portafilter and place it carefully on the scale
      3. Take the clear plastic funnel and place it inside the portafilter
      4. Pour the grinds inside - do not take the funnel off
      5. Hold the funnel+portafilter and tap against counter to level
      6. Remove funnel
      7. Place portafilter on counter
      8. Take tamper from magnetic holder on machine
      9. Compress grinds with tamper evenly
        1. Hold tamper like a doorknob
        2. Bump your hip up against the counter
        3. Make an L with your arm and press straight down until the grinds don't compress anymore
        4. Check the bed for level - the tamper shouldn't be tilted relative to the portafilter edge
      10. Tap grinds off tamper and put back in magnetic holder
  2. Water:
    1. Follow markings on machine to lock the portafilter into the group head
      1. Hold machine and twist hard - it should go past straight ahead by a few degrees then stop
    2. Put cup underneath both spouts
    3. Press rightmost (two cups) button
      1. This is programmed by me to do preinfusion and ~the right length of time for a "double shot"
      2. Machine will turn off pump on its own
  3. Pour:
    1. Take out cup
    2. Take out portafilter
    3. Press 2nd from right (one cup) button
      1. This is programmed by me to flow a little water to rinse the shower screen to get coffee off

Cleanup:

  • Turn off machine (leftmost button)
  • Bang portafilter against edge of trash can to dislodge the puck of grounds
  • Rinse remaining coffee off in sink
    • Rinse thoroughly, since there's coffee liquid underneath the filter basket too
    • You can pop out this filter basket for a deep clean. It clips right back in.
  • Leave to dry on a towel or on the drip tray
